What it does

One gesture, everything within reach.

Halo lives in your menu bar and over every app. It re-skins itself for whatever you're using — terminal, browser, editor — and you can shape every wheel yourself.

◎

Mouse-summoned

Hold a side, forward/back, or middle-click button. The ring blooms at the cursor; the app underneath keeps focus and receives the keystroke.

🎙

On-device dictation

Release at the center to dictate. WhisperKit transcribes entirely on your Mac — nothing leaves the machine. Finish-ring to Send, Send + Return, or Cancel.

✎

Visual Wheels editor

Design wheels on a live radial canvas: drag spokes to reorder, reshape the arc by hand, record shortcuts, nest sub-rings, and search the full SF Symbols catalog.

⌘

Scriptable

A spoke can run a shell command with your dictation in $HALO_TRANSCRIPT. Chain steps by saving outputs with as: name so one feeds the next.

✨

AI functions

Send your dictation to any OpenAI-compatible model — a local server or a cloud key — to clean up, translate, or answer it, with context from the app you're in. All defined in config.yaml.

◐

Liquid Glass

The wheel frosts the app behind it; the editor and windows use native macOS 26 Liquid Glass. Soft, optional UI sounds round it off.

⌥

Per-app profiles

Different wheels for different apps. Start a profile blank or as a copy of another. Everything lives in one hand-editable config.yaml — the source of truth.

How it works

Hold, flick, release.

01

Hold

Press and hold your summon button. A ring of spokes blooms at the cursor — no clicking, no targeting a tiny toolbar.

02

Flick

Sweep toward a spoke. Dwell on a well to open a sub-ring; the same spoke becomes Back in place. Pull into the empty wedge to cancel.

03

Release

Let go on a spoke to fire it, or at the center to dictate. The action lands in whatever app you were already using.

First launch: this build is signed with a self-signed certificate (not yet notarized), so macOS blocks it once. Try to open Halo, then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security and click Open Anyway next to Halo. Or skip the dance — run this in Terminal before launching:
xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Halo.app
Then grant Accessibility, Input Monitoring, and Microphone in Privacy & Security, and relaunch.
Requires macOS 14+. Liquid Glass visuals need macOS 26; older systems fall back to a solid dark look.
